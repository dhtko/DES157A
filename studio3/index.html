Instruction on Numeric Baseball

Numeric Baseball is a baseball-like game where you can be a pitcher without actually throwing a ball.
When you enter the game, you will see the strike zone, which is a hidden four-digit number.
Your objective (as a pitcher) is to guess and hit the strike zone. To throw a ball, you need to enter a
four-digit number WITHOUT REPEATING NUMBERS on the "Pitcher Up!" and press the "GO!" button.

After you throw a ball, the pitching result will be displayed on the "Record" board on the right of the game. The result comes in three categories: ball (correct number, but wrong position), strike (correct number and correct position), and out(wrong number). Based on how many balls, strikes, and outs you got, guess the strike zone and throw again. If your guess matches the strike zone within ten attempts (or inning in the game), you won the game!

That is all the information you need to enjoy this game. To check the instruction again, click the question mark in the top right corner..
